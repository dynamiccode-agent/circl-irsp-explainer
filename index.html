The Challenge

Insurance replacements are broken

When an insured appliance fails, the current process is fragmented across spreadsheets, phone calls, and disconnected systems — with no traceability, no rebate automation, and no sustainability tracking.

🔴

No single source of truth

Claims, orders, and compliance documents live in different systems. Insurers have no real-time visibility into fulfilment status — they wait for email updates.

⚠️

Rebates left unclaimed

Government energy efficiency schemes (VEU, ESS, STC) offer significant per-SKU rebates, but claiming them requires the right evidence chain — which nobody captures systematically.

🌱

Zero sustainability tracking

Every replacement job abates carbon — a qualified installer travels to a property and replaces a less-efficient appliance with a better one. This impact goes unmeasured and unmonetised.

Circl's Role
Builder / Operator — manages fulfilment, compliance, and rebate lodgement on behalf of the insurer
Allianz's Role
Insurer — lodges claims, monitors status in real time, receives rebate offsets on invoices
The Customer's Role
Signs a rebate authority form, allowing Circl to claim the government rebate while still benefiting from a discounted net cost
Claim Lifecycle

Eleven stages, one spine

Every claim travels a defined path from initial lodgement to final closure. Status transitions are role-aware and enforced by the server — no illegal jumps are possible.

📋 Lodged
Claim received, customer & property linked
✅ Eligibility Checked
3-step NCBA scheme verification
🔧 Allocated
Job assigned to trade org
⚙️ In Progress
Installer on-site, work begun
🛒 Draft Replacement
Product order being built
📦 Submitted
Order locked & sent to supplier
✍️ Awaiting Authority
Customer sign-off pending
📅 Scheduled
Installation date confirmed
🎉 Completed
Job done, C20 token issued
🔒 Closed
Invoice generated, rebate offset
💡
Status transitions are validated server-side using a CLAIM_TRANSITIONS matrix. Each role sees only the valid next steps for their permission level — a builder operator cannot jump a claim from Lodged directly to Completed.

Getting Started

Authentication & Organisations

IRSP uses Clerk for authentication with full Organisations support. Each real-world entity (Allianz, Circl) maps to a Clerk Organisation, with users invited into their relevant org with a role.

How Sign-In Works

1

Navigate to /sign-in

Clerk renders the sign-in UI. Supports email/password, magic link, and social OAuth. Your Clerk publishable key must be configured in env vars.

2

Select your organisation

After sign-in, Clerk's Organisation Switcher appears in the sidebar. Select your org (e.g. Allianz or Circl) — all data is scoped to this selection.

3

Role is resolved automatically

Your Clerk org role (org:builder_admin, org:insurer_viewer, etc.) is stripped and matched to the app's RBAC system. The sidebar nav and action buttons adjust accordingly.

4

Middleware enforces access

Every non-public route passes through Clerk middleware. Without a valid session, the request redirects to /sign-in. Customer routes (/c/*) bypass Clerk entirely — they use token auth.

Role Reference

platform_admin
Full access across all orgs. Manages schemes, products, users, adapters. Dynamic Code team only.
builder_admin
Full Circl operator access. Can allocate jobs, send customer links, manage all fulfilment actions.
builder_operator
Circl ops staff. Can update statuses, submit orders, upload compliance photos.
insurer_admin
Allianz admin. Can create claims and view all claims under the Allianz org.
insurer_viewer
Allianz read-only. Can view all Allianz claims but cannot mutate any state.
⚠️
Without Clerk keys, the app runs in dev passthrough mode — all requests resolve as platform_admin to allow smoke testing without credentials.

Fulfilment

Jobs & trade allocation

When a claim is allocated, a matching Job record is created at /builder/jobs. Jobs track the physical installation work, separate from the claim itself.

Job Lifecycle

1

Pending Allocation

Job created automatically when claim moves to Allocated. No trade assigned yet.

2

Allocated

Builder admin manually assigns to a trade organisation. Logged in job_allocations audit table.

3

Scheduled → In Progress → Completed

Status progresses as the installer confirms date, starts work, and finishes. On Completed, a C20 carbon token is issued automatically.

GPS Compliance Photos

Completion requires a set of compliance photos per category (HVAC/Solar = 3 photos, Appliances = 2). Each photo is checked for:

  • 📍GPS coordinates — must be within 200m of property address
  • 🕒Timestamp — must be within last 24 hours
  • 🤖AI validation — stub returns passed:true (real model post-MVP)
🔧
GPS Override: iOS/browser metadata stripping is common. Admins can override GPS failures with a typed reason — the override is audit-logged with the actor's user ID.

Customer Authority

Rebate sign-off flow

Before any government rebate can be lodged, the customer must assign their rebate entitlement to Circl. This is captured via a secure tokenised link that bypasses Clerk entirely.

1

Generate the access link

A builder_admin or builder_operator clicks "Generate & copy customer link" on the claim detail. The API creates a 32-byte hex token with a 14-day expiry and returns the URL.

2

Customer receives the link

The URL is in the form /c/[token]. No login required. The customer sees their property, the replacement products selected, and the rebate amount being assigned to Circl.

3

Sign the physical form

The customer prints and physically signs the authority form. They then photograph the signed document and upload it through the portal. A photo of the physical signature is the legal record — digital-only is not sufficient per the brief.

4

Sign or Refuse

The customer types their full name to confirm, or provides a refusal reason. Either action is audit-logged. If refused, the claim proceeds but all rebate amounts are zeroed out downstream.

Compliance Engine

3-step eligibility verification

Before any rebate can be applied, three sequential checks run against the NCBA adapter. Each result is persisted as an eligibility_checks row — creating a full audit trail visible on the claim detail.

Check 1 — Scheme Active
Is there an active rebate scheme for this product category, in this state, on this date? Checks the schemes table via the NCBA adapter.
Check 2 — No Prior Claim
Has this property address already claimed this scheme? The property is identified by a SHA-256 hash of the normalised address — prevents double-claiming.
Check 3 — SKU Efficiency
Does the selected product SKU meet the scheme's minimum energy rating? For example, VEU may require A++ or better for HVAC units to qualify.
🔌
All three checks route through the NCBA adapter interface (lib/adapters/ncba.ts). Currently mocked — reads from the local schemes table. Setting NCBA_ADAPTER=live in env vars swaps in the real API implementation with no code changes required elsewhere.
// lib/adapters/_types.ts — locked interface
export interface NCBARebatePort {
  checkActiveScheme(input: {
    product_category: ProductCategory;
    state: AUState;
    date: Date;
  }): Promise<{ active: boolean; scheme_id?: string }>;

  checkPriorClaim(input: {
    property_address_hash: string; // SHA-256 of normalised address
    scheme_id: string;
  }): Promise<{ already_claimed: boolean }>;

  checkProductEligibility(input: {
    sku: string;
    scheme_id: string;
  }): Promise<{ eligible: boolean; rebate_cents?: number }>;
}
Sustainability

C20 Carbon Token issuance

Every completed job automatically generates a C20 carbon abatement token. The token is assigned to the insurer organisation and represents a measurable CO₂e reduction from the replacement.

Weight < 50

1,200 kg CO₂e
Weight 50–150
e.g. Install job · 80 km travel
Large
2,400 kg CO₂e
Weight > 150
e.g. Install job · 120+ km travel

The Formula

// lib/services/carbon.ts — formula_version: "placeholder-2026-05"
const CATEGORY_WEIGHT = {
  "Install":       1.5,  // HVAC, Hot Water, Solar, Battery
  "Replace":       1.0,  // Appliance drop-in swap
  "Purchase Only": 0.3,  // TV, dishwasher, cooktop
};

const weight = CATEGORY_WEIGHT[job_category] * travel_distance_km;
const size = weight < 50 ? "small" : weight < 150 ? "medium" : "large";
🌍
Tokens are issued automatically via issueTokenForJob() when a job's status moves to Completed. The tokenRef format is C20-JOB-XXXX-[timestamp36]. A blockchainTxHash field is reserved for future on-chain registration.
Administration

Schemes, rebates & products

The /admin portal is restricted to platform_admin role. It manages the rebate programmes and product catalogue that power the eligibility engine.

Each scheme can have per-SKU rebate amounts set by the admin. For example, SKU A01 (Daikin 2.5kW) under VEU carries a $124.00 rebate. These amounts are shown live in the product picker when a scheme matches the claim's state and category.
