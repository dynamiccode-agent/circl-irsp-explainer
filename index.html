Platform Overview

Insurance Replacement Service Platform

CIRCL IRSP is a multi-portal workflow platform connecting builders, insurers, and homeowners to manage energy-efficient product replacement jobs under insurance schemes. Claims flow from lodgement through eligibility checking, product selection, installation, and customer sign-off to closure.

Builders lodge claims, select eligible products, assign jobs to trade installers, and track compliance through to closure.

Builder Portal

Creating a Claim

New claims are lodged from the Claims page. The builder enters the customer's details, the property address, and selects the applicable insurance scheme. On submission, an eligibility check runs automatically via the NCBA adapter.

1

Enter customer details

First name, last name, email, and phone. A customer record is created or matched within your organisation.

2

Add the property

Street address where installation will occur. Each property is tied to a customer record and checked for prior claims.

3

Select insurer & scheme

Choose the policy's insurer and applicable replacement scheme. The scheme determines which products are eligible and the rebate rates applied.

4

Eligibility check runs automatically

The NCBA adapter verifies the scheme is active, checks for prior claims at the property, and validates SKU efficiency. Claim status moves to Eligibility Check until the check passes.

Customer Authority

Before the replacement order can be submitted to the insurer, the customer must grant authority to proceed. The builder generates a secure token link from the claim. The customer receives it by email or SMS and signs off via a no-login portal — no account required.

1

Builder sends the authority request

Clicking "Send Authority Request" in the claim detail creates a 32-byte hex token stored against the customer with a 14-day expiry.

2

Customer receives the link

An email or SMS containing a unique URL such as circl.app/c/a3f8d9… — no password needed to access it.

3

Customer reviews and grants authority

The portal shows what was installed, the insurer, and the environmental impact. One button to grant authority — claim status moves to Authority Received.

4

Builder submits the replacement order

With authority confirmed, the builder submits the order to the insurer. The insurer can see the completed claim in their portal.

Customer Portal

Customer Portal

Customers never need to create an account or remember a password. Their portal is a token-authenticated page — the URL itself is the key. It's intentionally minimal: show what was installed, confirm the insurer, and capture the authority sign-off.

Token auth: The server validates the 32-byte hex token in the URL path, checks it hasn't expired (14-day window), and loads the claim — no Clerk session required. Tokens are stored hashed and tied to the customer record.
